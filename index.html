What is a Bunion?

A bunion forms when your big‑toe joint drifts out of alignment, creating the painful bump on the side of your foot.

The medical term is hallux valgus. Misalignment can cause persistent pain, especially in tight shoes.

Left untreated, bunions may trigger hammertoes, calluses, and chronic discomfort that limits your daily activities.

Treatment Options

Non-Surgical Options

  • Appropriate footwear with wider toe boxes
  • Custom orthotics and toe spacers
  • Bunion pads and splints
  • Physical therapy exercises
  • Anti-inflammatory medication
  • Ice therapy for pain management

Non-surgical treatments can provide relief and slow progression but typically don't reverse the deformity.

Surgical Options

  • Minimally invasive bunionectomy
  • Lapidus procedure (fusion of joints)
  • Chevron osteotomy (bone realignment)
  • Akin procedure (phalangeal correction)
  • Scarf osteotomy (for severe cases)

Surgery is typically considered when conservative treatments haven't provided adequate relief or the deformity is severe.
